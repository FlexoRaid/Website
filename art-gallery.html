Astrophotography

Teide, photographed at an altitude of approximately 2,000 meters, captured at night using a long exposure of around 25 seconds. The image reveals the stunning interplay of darkness and light, with the subtle glow of the stars and the serene atmosphere of the high-altitude landscape. The extended exposure allows the camera to capture details that are invisible to the naked eye, creating a sense of depth and motion in the night sky. This technique emphasizes the majesty of Teide and the quiet, almost otherworldly beauty of its nocturnal environment.

-Roman Przewlocki
